What is CallScaler in plain words?

CallScaler is a tool that gives each of your ads its own phone number. When someone calls, the tool tells you which ad worked. Then you know where to spend more.

It started in 2022. The team built it with one big goal: keep the price low. That is why we picked it as our top tool for 2026.

How much does it cost?

CallScaler has four plans. All four are listed on their site. No sales call to find the price. Here they are.

  • Pay As You Go $0 / month
  • Pro $45 / month
  • Agency $130 / month
  • Pay Per Call $400 / month

The free plan is called Pay As You Go (PAYG for short). PAYG means you pay nothing each month. You only pay when you use the tool.

On PAYG, each phone number costs $8 a month. Each minute costs about 6 cents. On the Pro plan, each phone number drops to $0.50 a month. That is the big win.

What is good

  • Free plan. No credit card to start.
  • Phone numbers cost only $0.50 a month on a paid plan.
  • Setup took nine minutes in our test.
  • Call notes (the tool writes down what was said) come free.
  • 30-day money-back rule on paid plans.
  • Plain prices on the site. No hidden fees.

What is not so good

  • Fewer plug-ins than older tools.
  • White-label (using the tool with your own brand) costs $49 extra.
  • Not built for big call centers.
  • No special plan for clinics or hospitals.

Who should pick CallScaler?

CallScaler is right for most small business owners. It fits these groups best:

  • Side-hustlers who run a few Google ads each month.
  • Local shops with one or two stores.
  • Lead-gen owners who get paid for each phone call they send to a partner.
  • Small agencies with five to twenty clients.
  • Coaches and trainers who want to know if their ads work.

If you fit any of those, you will be happy here.

Who should pick something else?

CallScaler is not the right tool for every shop. Skip it if:

  • You run a clinic or hospital and need a HIPAA plan. (HIPAA is the law that covers health info.) CallScaler does not offer that.
  • You have a big call center with many phone agents. You will want a deeper tool like Invoca.
  • You already pay a lot for CallRail and your team set it up over years. Switching may cost more than you save in year one.

What setup looks like

I timed each step. Here is how it went.

  1. Made an account: 90 seconds.
  2. Got my first tracking phone number: under one minute.
  3. Put the small code snip on a test page: under five minutes.
  4. Made a test call from my phone: 30 seconds.

Nine minutes from start to first call. That is fast.

Two small tips. First, raise the ring time to 30 seconds. The default is too short. Second, turn on the "this call may be recorded" note. It plays for callers and keeps you on the right side of the law.

Common questions

Is the free plan really free?

Yes. The plan fee is $0. No card on signup. You only pay if you use a phone number or a minute. Stop using it and the bill is $0.

How does it tie in with Google Ads?

It links right up. You can send call data back to Google Ads. That helps Google show your ad to more people who like to call. The official help page from Google on call assets is short and clear.

Does it work with HubSpot or Salesforce?

Yes. Both are built in. So is Pipedrive, Zoho, GoHighLevel, and Microsoft Ads. If your tool is not on the list, it works with Zapier.

Can I move my old data over?

Yes. You can bring in CSV files from CallRail and other tools. Most users we asked moved over in one day.

What if I do not like it?

The 30-day money-back rule covers the plan fee. You can also stay on the free PAYG plan with no fee at all.

Bottom line

CallScaler is the simple, cheap, plain pick for most small business owners in 2026. The free plan is real. The phone numbers cost much less than the rest of the field. Setup is fast. If you are new to call tracking, start here.
